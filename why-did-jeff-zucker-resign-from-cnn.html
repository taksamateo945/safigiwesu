He was chosen executive producer of Today in January 1992 at the age of 26, making him the program’s youngest executive producer ever.

He had proved his competence as the morning show Today soared to the top of the ratings under his direction. And it remained there throughout his tenure. Likewise, in December 2000, he was appointed President of NBC Entertainment.

Within three years, he was successfully named as President of the NBC Entertainment, News, and Cable Group. From May 2004, he served as president of NBC Universal Television Group, ascending to CEO in December 2005.

Later, he also co-created, and executive produced Katie, a syndicated daytime show featuring journalist Katie Couric, after leaving NBC. He did, however, leave the show to become the head of CNN Worldwide in 2013.
