Educational Background

Dr. Fauci attended Regis High School in New York City. He studied at the College of the Holy Cross and obtained his M.D. from Cornell University Medical College in 1966.

Career Background

Following his M.D., Dr. Fauci completed an internship and residency at The New York Hospital-Cornell Medical Center.

Soon after, he started working as a clinical associate in the Laboratory of Clinical Investigation in the National Institutes of Health.

After six years, he was appointed as the Head of the Clinical Physiology Section and became Chief of the Laboratory of Immunoregulation in 1980.

Dr. Fauci has been the Director of NIAID since 1984. He has held that position for over 36 years.
