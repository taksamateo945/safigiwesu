Russell Crowe's Latest Social Media Activity Helps To Highlight New Zealand's Entertainment Industry

Actors are no strangers to social media. They know better than anyone else that platforms like Twitter and Instagram allow them to build their fan base outside of the cinema. Yet, some actors have chosen to stay offline, such as Cillian Murphy, who has starred in the BBC’s Peaky Blinders since 2013. It makes sense, though: Murphy is a Golden Globe-nominated actor who often portrays darker, more serious roles, so it’s on-brand for the Irishman.

Many other serious actors seem to also avoid social media. One that comes to mind is Russell Crowe, aged 57. The New Zealand-born actor (whose net worth is around $100 million) has played many roles, but he is best known for his dramas, such as Les Misérables, American Gangster, and Gladiator, an epic historical drama blockbuster directed by British film director and producer Sir Ridley Scott. Surprisingly, the global actor has been active on social media recently. He’s been filming a new project in Malta and has been teasing and engaging with his 2.7 million Twitter followers, posting mysterious shots of set locations.

It’s a great example of social media acceptance and adoption in terms of age group and other demographics. However, it also brings up interesting questions about New Zealand’s film industry. By far, Crowe is one of the most famous faces to come from the island, and his recent activity on social media has helped shine a light on the country’s own film career, which has faced setbacks over the last few months.

The Latest From New Zealand

In August, Amazon Studios moved its flagship series for the Lord of the Rings franchise from New Zealand to the United Kingdom. It’s a blow to New Zealand, which has built a profitable entertainment industry on the franchise’s success, so it’s no surprise that Amazon’s decision has not been well received. However, there are reasons for optimism. Many other movies and shows will be filmed there, and there are other sectors in New Zealand’s entertainment industry that are performing well. 

In its publishing sector, domestic revenue from NZ content increased to $52.6 million in 2020.
